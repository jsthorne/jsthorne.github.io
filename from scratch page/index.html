    2. Real Steel

    I don’t care what people say about this movie. Call me a kid but it’s been five stars for me since the day I watched it for the first time when I was 10 and this will be five stars until the day I die. Makes me so emotionally every time.

    Charlie Kenton is an absolute prick of a guy but the movie still manages to make you empathize with him. When Max calls him dad at the end it’s still good enough to make me cry.

    This movie makes me feel so many things and it will never cease to be one of my favorites of all time.

    5. The Dark Knight

    Masterpiece.

    This is easily one of the greatest movies of all time. It is almost “knight” and day (pun intended) between this and its prequel.

    Easily the greatest part about this movie is that it NEVER slows down. It jumps from one insane scene directly to another and each one gives me the chills.

    Heath Ledger’s acting is the greatest performance of all time (RIP king). He literally embodies the Joker in every way.

    The little details from Harvey’s coin to the FIRE one liners to the camera angles just adds to how iconic and insane this movie is.

    I have 0 critique. Phenomenal experience every time. Would (and did) watch 6 times in a month.

  6. _______________

    6. RRR

    I am convinced that Ramaraju and Bheem could take out the Avengers.

    One of the most frequent problems movies face is they try to be too much while succeeding to be too little. RRR tries to be everything. The only difference is it succeeds by a massive margin to do it all. I can’t think of another movie that would dare try to be a rom com, historical drama, musical, and action movie, not to mention succeeding in doing all four. RRR is a movie about friendship, brotherhood, loyalty, strength, forgiveness, justice, love, courage, nobility, family and good old fashioned revenge.

    Never have I ever seen a movie take 45 minutes JUST to introduce its plot. RRR is one of the boldest films I have ever seen, and it makes 3 hours feel like 20 minutes.

    This movie will make you cry and it will make you laugh. It will make you scream and then cheer. It will make you jump out of your seat and leave you in shock more than any movie you have seen before. One minute you’re speechless, the next it’s all you can talk about. RRR will live in your mind rent free both while you’re awake and while you’re sleeping. It’s what you think about falling asleep and waking up. It will leave you saying, “there’s no way they just did that.”

    Yes. Yes they did.

    8. Baby Driver

    I generally only have fun watching movies with people, but I watched this alone and it was still one of the funnest times I’ve had watching a movie in the last several years.

    I love music. I love action. I love cars. I love Lily James. This movie was just shy of perfect.

    The way the gunshots and car sounds and dialogue and explosions and even the wall GRAFITTI all went with the music made me feel like an 11 year old kid watching the Phineas and Ferb Tree House Fight episode, I was just giddy the whole time.

    Only thing for me was some aspects of the plot felt a little hollow. Deborah’s motives seemed very far fetched, and she seemed to be unfazed going from a diner waitress to witnessing several murders in one night.

    Other than that, this movie made me audibly gasp several times and jump out of my bed more than once. Absolutely phenomenal film.

    10. How to Train Your Dragon

    One of the greatest animated films of all time. Say what you want but I will hold to it.

    The perfect pacing, impactful story presentation, emotional roller coaster, the bookend phrasing, dynamic relationship between characters, and the MASTERFUL thematically written music. Though slightly dated, this film remains one not only relevant but loved and ICONIC. It has a beautiful balance between conflict and resolution and the climax makes me feel more than I feel in the first 20 minutes of Up.

    John Powell is one of the most underrated composers of our generation. The subtle uses of Hiccup’s and Toothless’s themes that correspond with the aspects of the scene in “Test Flight” are PEAK thematic writing. Powell uses each theme in different instances while changing it slightly such as changing it to minor to incite different emotions. It’s just brilliant.

    This is a masterpiece. Honestly might even be 5 stars…
